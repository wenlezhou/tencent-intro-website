## About this course

This course empowers faculty, instructional designers, and educational leaders to apply the 4D Framework to their own teaching practice and institutional strategy.

At Anthropic, we believe that empowering people with AI requires engaging with a wide range of human perspectives and experiences. This course empowers participants to apply the 4D Framework to their own educational practice. Participants will learn to apply the 4D Framework to their own teaching practice, using AI as a thinking partner to enhance course design, create coherent learning materials, and develop authentic assessments while modeling responsible AI collaboration for their students.

This course is the result of a long partnership between Anthropic and professors Rick Dakan and Joseph Feller. It builds on their experiences in training fellow educators and teaching the AI Fluency Framework to their students, and on feedback and questions that arose from the earlier course: AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations.

The work was supported in part by the Higher Education Authority (Ireland) through the National Forum for the Enhancement of Teaching and Learning.

## Key Concepts for Educators

### The 4D Framework in Education

1. **Delegation** - Knowing when to delegate tasks to AI (and when not to)
2. **Description** - Crafting effective prompts for educational content
3. **Discernment** - Critically evaluating AI outputs for accuracy and appropriateness
4. **Diligence** - Responsible and ethical AI use in educational contexts

### Applications in Teaching

- **Course Design** - Use AI as a thinking partner to enhance course structure
- **Learning Materials** - Create coherent, high-quality materials with AI assistance
- **Assessments** - Develop authentic assessments that evaluate student learning
- **Modeling** - Demonstrate responsible AI collaboration for students
