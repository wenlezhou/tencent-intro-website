## About this course

This course empowers builders to develop AI fluency — increasing impact and efficiency while staying true to what it means to own the full arc from problem to shipped solution.

At Anthropic, we believe that empowering people with AI requires engaging with a wide range of human perspectives and experiences. Builders — product people, engineers, and makers — are uniquely positioned to leverage AI to amplify what they create—but only if they can approach it intentionally, with clear judgment and purpose.

This course helps builders develop practical AI collaboration skills through the 4D Framework (Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence). In this course, we'll explore how the framework applies to real builder tasks—from framing a customer problem to shipping a product—and consider what it means to integrate AI responsibly across the full arc of building.

This course is built in partnership with CodePath, drawing on research with engineers and product builders about their actual needs, concerns, and aspirations for AI adoption.

The work builds on our *AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations* course, adapted specifically for the builder context where speed of implementation and the human role in judgment create unique considerations for AI collaboration.

## Key Concepts for Builders

### The 4D Framework in Builder Context

1. **Delegation** — Knowing when to delegate tasks to AI (and when to keep implementation in your own hands)
2. **Description** — Crafting effective prompts for code generation, debugging, and system design
3. **Discernment** — Critically evaluating AI-generated code for correctness, security, and maintainability
4. **Diligence** — Responsible and ethical AI use in building processes

### Applications for Builders

- **Problem Framing** — Use AI to clarify customer problems and explore solution spaces
- **Code Generation** — Generate code faster, but with critical oversight
- **Debugging** — Use AI to identify and fix issues, with human verification
- **User Experience** — Design and iterate on UX with AI assistance
- **Shipping** — Own the full arc from problem to shipped solution, with AI as a tool (not a replacement for judgment)

### Unique Considerations for Builders

- **Speed of Implementation** — Balancing fast iteration with code quality
- **Human Role in Judgment** — Knowing where your expertise is essential vs. where AI can help
- **Full Arc Ownership** — Taking responsibility for the complete build process
- **Code Quality & Security** — Ensuring AI-generated code meets your standards
- **User-Centered Design** — Keeping user needs at the center, with AI as support

## The Builder's Workflow with AI

### 1. Problem Framing (Description)
- Use AI to explore the problem space
- Clarify customer needs and constraints
- Generate and evaluate potential approaches

### 2. Solution Design (Delegation + Description)
- Delegate routine implementation to AI
- Keep architecture and critical decisions for yourself
- Use AI to explore trade-offs and alternatives

### 3. Implementation (Delegation + Discernment)
- Generate code with AI assistance
- Apply discernment: review, test, and refine the generated code
- Iterate until the solution meets your standards

### 4. Testing & Validation (Discernment + Diligence)
- Use AI to generate test cases
- Apply discernment: verify that tests are meaningful and coverage is adequate
- Ensure edge cases are handled

### 5. Shipping & Iteration (Diligence)
- Own the final decision to ship
- Monitor post-launch and iterate with AI assistance
- Document what you learned for future projects

## Best Practices for Builders Using AI

1. **Stay in the loop** — You own the full arc from problem to shipped solution
2. **Use AI for speed, not as a crutch** — Build your own understanding alongside AI assistance
3. **Verify everything** — AI-generated code, tests, and designs need human validation
4. **Build discernment** — The more you understand, the better you'll be at evaluating AI outputs
5. **Document your process** — Capture what works and what doesn't for future reference
6. **Share with your team** — Help others learn effective AI collaboration patterns

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Over-delegation** — Delegating tasks you don't understand (you won't be able to discern the output quality)
2. **Under-discernment** — Accepting AI-generated code without proper review
3. **Vibe coding** — Building on vibes rather than understanding (you won't be able to maintain or debug later)
4. **Skiping edge cases** — AI tends to generate happy-path code; you need to ensure robustness
5. **Ignoring security** — AI-generated code may not follow security best practices; review carefully
