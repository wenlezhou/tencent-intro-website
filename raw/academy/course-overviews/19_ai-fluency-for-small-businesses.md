## About this course

This course empowers small businesses to develop AI fluency in order to increase organizational impact and efficiency while staying true to their mission and values.

At Anthropic, we believe that empowering people with AI requires engaging with a wide range of human perspectives and experiences. Small businesses are uniquely positioned to leverage AI to advance their mission—but only if they can approach it intentionally, with resilience and clear purpose.

This course helps small business staff—whether in customer interactions, back-office management, supply chain operations, or leadership—build practical AI collaboration skills through the 4D Framework (Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence). In this course, we'll explore how the framework applies to common small business tasks, and consider what it means to implement AI across a small business.

This course is the result of a partnership between Anthropic, PayPal, Prospect Butcher, and MAKS Enterprises TIPM Rebuilders, drawing on research with small business professionals about their actual needs, concerns, and aspirations for AI adoption.

The work builds on our *AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations* course, adapted specifically for the small business context where limited resources, business accountabilities, and customer experience create unique considerations for AI collaboration.

## Key Concepts for Small Businesses

### The 4D Framework in Small Business Context

1. **Delegation** — Knowing when to delegate tasks to AI (while maintaining quality and customer trust)
2. **Description** — Crafting effective prompts for business communications and operations
3. **Discernment** — Critically evaluating AI outputs for accuracy, brand alignment, and customer appropriateness
4. **Diligence** — Responsible and ethical AI use in small business contexts

### Applications in Small Business

- **Customer Interactions** — Use AI to enhance (not replace) customer service
- **Back-Office Management** — Streamline operations, invoicing, and record-keeping
- **Supply Chain Operations** — Optimize inventory, logistics, and vendor management
- **Leadership & Strategy** — Use AI for market research, forecasting, and decision support
- **Marketing & Communications** — Create compelling content while maintaining authentic brand voice

### Unique Considerations for Small Businesses

- **Limited Resources** — Doing more with less using AI tools
- **Business Accountabilities** — Maintaining customer trust and regulatory compliance
- **Customer Experience** — Ensuring AI use enhances (not diminishes) customer relationships
- **Team Dynamics** — Introducing AI in ways that empower (not threaten) your team
- **Mission & Values** — Ensuring AI use aligns with your business identity
